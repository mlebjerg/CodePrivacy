Social Media

Facebook's privacy policies reinforce the message that "you have control over who sees what you share on Facebook." But if you use Facebook at all, you don't have much control over what Facebook itself sees about you.

Who controls our data?

The EU has had a data-protection directive since 1995,
yet studies have repeatedly shown that its rights
weren't well-enforced. The GDPR has been law since
2016, yet it only grew teeth this May, with companies
now open to fines of up to 4 percent of global annual
revenue. Especially big companies still collecting user
data by their social media, it could use for business
analysis or even give to third party.

People can find our information
without knowing us

Through FB, IG, Twitter we’ve already posted on, people
might be able to find our past and now. Or friends may
put us at risk on social networks, even if we decide to
delete our social media account, our profile is still
“encoded” in previous interactions with our friends.
Our friends are leaving telltale clues about us, what we like, and even how we
are likely to vote in any election.
